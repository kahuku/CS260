My Current Research Topics at BYU's CSRL

GANs for Password List Generation

The former director of the CSRL, Justin Giboney, published a paper on using information gathered by OSINT (open source intelligence) to generate passwords lists that would be potential current passwords the study participants may be using. I propose to further this research by using a Generative Aversarial Network to generate potential passwords for hash cracking with higher accuracy.

Gamification for Enhancing Undergraduate Cybersecurity Education

My team and I at the CSRL wrote a research proposal on the effects of gamification in undergraduate cybersecurity education--specificaly, how the use of Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions can be used to foster positive emotional responses during the difficult stages of the learning process.

We submitted our proposal to the Organized Cybersecurity Journal in November. We are still waiting to be assigned an editor, but in the time since the paper has been sent out for review, the BYU CSA club hosted an end-of-semester CTF, from which we were able to collect survey responses to begin anaylzing to determine a trend between experience in cybersecurity and positive vs negative emotions felt, and hopefully we will be able to prove that gamified learning encourages participation from less experienced students.
